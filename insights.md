#Key Insights

## 1. Player Distribution
This insight calculated the total number of registered male players who played during the Spring 18+ season for USTA Norcal. We found that most male players for the USTA Norcal section fall within the 3.5 to 4.5 rating range, which indicates a mid-skill to advanced player population for NorCal. The 3.5, 4.0, and 4.5 rating trio compromise 81.7% of the NorCal player population. The smallest rating level was the highly advanced 5.5 rating, which consisted of only 10 players out of the total player population of 4,986 players, or 0.2% of the player population.


## 2. Elite Players
5.0 rated players are very rare and are concentrated in a small amount of cities, with San Francisco and San Jose having the highest concentration of 5.0 rated players. San Francisco and San Jose account for 32% of the 5.0 rated registered players in Northern California. 5.5 rated players are even more rare than 5.0s.


##3. Geographic Trends
Most cities with large populations have the most amount of players, with SF, San Jose, Oakland, and Sacramento making the top 5 cities with the most players. These 4 cities have a population with at least over 400,000 residents. 
Danville is an exception- Danville is ranked 4th on total registered players but has a population of only 43,850. One reason for this is Danville is a wealthy suburban area, and tennis is an expensive sport. Danville residents can afford club memberships, league registration fees, tennis lessons, and tournament play. This shows that player concentration is driven by demographics, access, and engagement. 


##4. Data Quality
Duplicate, null, and inconsistent records were identified, removed, and resolved before conducting this analysis, which helps ensure the accuracy and reliability of these results.
